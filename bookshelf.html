Resume

Bookshelf

Technical references that shaped my work, plus fiction and other reads worth returning to.

Probabilistic Robotics

Thrun, Burgard, and Fox

Bayes filters, Kalman family estimators, and particle filters, the backbone of robust localization.

Multiple View Geometry in Computer Vision

Hartley and Zisserman

The standard reference for multi-view geometry, calibration, and bundle adjustment intuition.

State Estimation for Robotics

Timothy D. Barfoot

Clear treatment of Lie groups, rotation estimation, and modern robotics state estimation.

Convex Optimization

Boyd and Vandenberghe

Useful whenever perception pipelines reduce to structured nonlinear optimization problems.

Deep Learning

Goodfellow, Bengio, and Courville

Broad foundations for the learning components that sit next to geometric pipelines.

Atomic Habits

James Clear

Small habits, systems over goals, and the compounding of incremental improvement.

Ready Player One

Ernest Cline

A pop-culture-soaked hunt through a vast virtual world, pure escapist fun.

The Three Body Problem

Liu Cixin

Hard SF, cosmic stakes, and ideas that stay with you long after the last page.

Red Rising

Pierce Brown

Fast-paced rebellion and intrigue across a stratified future society.

Children of Time

Adrian Tchaikovsky

Deep-time evolution, non-human minds, and a universe that feels genuinely alien.

Children of Ruin

Adrian Tchaikovsky

A worthy sequel that widens the canvas and doubles down on weird, wonderful biology.

Kite Runner

Khaled Hosseini

Guilt, redemption, and friendship set against a changing Afghanistan.

A Thousand Splendid Suns

Khaled Hosseini

Interwoven lives and resilience under impossible circumstances.

Twilight series

Stephenie Meyer

Small-town supernatural romance, sometimes you want exactly that mood.

Harry Potter series

J. K. Rowling

The comfort read: friendship, courage, and a fully imagined world to get lost in.

Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind

Yuval Noah Harari

Provides a sweeping overview of human history, from the Stone Age to the 21st century, blending biology, anthropology, and history
